# Grace Relationship to Etta

Version: v1.0
Classification: Foundational
Role: Inter-System Relationship Definition

---

# 1. Introduction

Etta and Grace were not created to compete.

They were created to complete each other.

Both systems occupy different positions within the same cognitive cycle.

Neither replaces the other.

Neither dominates the other.

Together they enable the transition from understanding to reality.

---

# 2. The Core Difference

The simplest distinction is:

```text
Etta expands.

Grace converges.
```

Etta increases possibility.

Grace reduces possibility into decisions.

Both actions are necessary.

Without expansion, systems stagnate.

Without convergence, systems drift.

---

# 3. Etta

Etta is a cognitive expansion architecture.

Its responsibilities include:

* knowledge preservation,
* context retention,
* pattern discovery,
* cognitive continuity,
* exploration,
* synthesis.

Etta asks:

> What exists?

> What could exist?

> What relationships exist?

Etta seeks understanding.

---

# 4. Grace

Grace is a realization architecture.

Its responsibilities include:

* execution planning,
* artifact governance,
* state management,
* closure,
* canonicalization,
* reconciliation.

Grace asks:

> What should exist now?

> What becomes reality?

> What survives?

Grace seeks commitment.

---

# 5. Expansion and Convergence

A cognitive system without expansion becomes rigid.

A cognitive system without convergence becomes chaotic.

The healthy cycle is:

```text
Expansion
↓
Understanding
↓
Decision
↓
Execution
↓
Closure
```

Etta owns expansion.

Grace owns closure.

---

# 6. Knowledge and Reality

Knowledge and reality are not equivalent.

Knowledge describes possibility.

Reality describes commitment.

Etta preserves knowledge.

Grace preserves outcomes.

Etta creates understanding.

Grace is primarily oriented toward:

analysis without closure
